The Immune System is a complicated battlefield.

This illustrates the basics.

Mage - Lymphocyte

Skills:

Cast: Spawn

Secretes antibodies, which have a complementary shape to the antigens on the surface of the pathogenic cell. They bind to the antigens and cause them to clump together (agglutination).

Cast: Relief

Antitoxins neutralise toxins released by pathogens. These toxins can disrupt normal cellular processes, cause damage to cells and tissues.

Warrior - phagocyte

Skills:

Cast: Bludgeon

Violently "engulfs and digests (phagocytosis)" nearby pathogens with its blade. Pathogens are located as phagocytes can detect can detect chemicals produced by pathogenic cells.

Cast: Recycle

*Less violently "engulfs and digests" dead or damaged cells preventing the release of cellular debris and other harmful substances that can damage surrounding tissues and lead to inflammation.

Goblin - bacteria

Skills:

Cast: Poison

Secretes toxins which greatly inhibit cells and can cause bacterial infections. Manipulate host cell functions and takes over the control of vital processes.

Passives:

Resist

Overuse and misuse of antibiotics can lead to the evolution of antibiotic-resistant bacteria, making it difficult to treat infections.

Ghoul - virus

Skills:

Cast: Hijack

Corrupts nearby cells by injecting its genetic information into them. Causes them to function abnormally and can also lead to the release of new viral cells that can infect other cells..

Passives:

Virus

Viruses are hard to kill because they are not considered living organisms. They lack cellular structures that can be targeted by antibiotics or other conventional medicines.

Hijacked

Cell has been infected by a Ghoul (virus)

Effects:

Hijacked

Hijacks the host cell's ability to replicate itself. This can cause the host to function abnormally or die, and can also lead to the release of new viral particles that can infect other cells.

Poisoned

Cell has been infected by a Goblin (bacteria)

Effects:

Poisoned

Can lead to cell death or mutations that can result in diseases such as cancer. The severity of the effect of toxins on cells depends on factors such as the type of toxin, the dose, and the duration of exposure.

Tagged

Cell has been tagged by an antibody released by mage/archmages (lymphocytes/memory cells)

Effects:

Tagged

Antibodies coat the pathogen, making it easier for immune cells like phagocytes to recognize and engulf it. Furthermore, slows pathogens down.
